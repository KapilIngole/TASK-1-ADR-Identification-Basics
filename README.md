# TASK-1-ADR-Identification-Basics
ADR Identification Basics Task - Pharmacovigilance Internship
# Objective 
Analyze the patient case to determine whether the observed symptoms represent an Adverse Drug Reaction (ADR) and identify the suspected drug.
# Patient Information
- Gender: Male
- Age: 62 years
# Presenting Symptoms
- Persisten Dry Cough
- Dizziness
- Fatigue
# Medication History
Lisinopril (started 3 weeks ago)

Metformin (Taken regularly for diabetes)
# Timeline
| Day | Event | 
| :--- | :---: 
| Day 21 | Started Lisinopril | 
| Day 10 | Dry cough started | 
| Day 5 | Diziness and Fatigue Started |
| Day 0 | Symptoms continue |
# Analysis
The patient's symptoms developed after initiating Lisinopril, indicating a clear temporal association between drug exposure and symptom onset.

A persistent dry cough is a well-known adverse effect of ACE inhibitors such as Lisinopril. The accompanying dizziness may be related to the blood pressure-lowering effect of the medication. Metformin is less likely to be responsible, as it is not commonly associated with a persistent dry cough.
# Conclusion
ADR Detetcted: Detected ✅

Drug responsible: Lisinopril

Reason: 
The onset of persistent dry cough, dizziness, and fatigue following the initiation of Lisinopril strongly suggests a probable Lisinopril-induced Adverse Drug Reaction.
# Learning Outcome
This exercise highlights the importance of assessing:
- Patient symptoms
- Medication history
- Temporal relationship between medication use and symptom onset
- Known adverse effects of the suspected drug
